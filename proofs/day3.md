Therefore, 


a + b = 3m + 3n


-> a + b = 3(m + n)

 
Since n and m are both integers, their sum, m + n, must also be an integer. Since m + n is an integer, 3(m + n) is divisible by 3. 


Since 3(m + n) is divisible by 3, and a + b is equal to 3(m + n), a + b must also be divisible by 3.
