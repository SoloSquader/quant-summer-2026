## Problem 1

A 5-card hand is drawn from a standard 52-card deck.

Question: What is the probability that the hand contains exactly 1 ace?

Answer: 29.947%

Result: Correct.


## Problem 2

A 5-card hand is drawn from a standard 52-card deck.

Question: What is the probability that the hand contains at least 1 ace?

Answer: 34.1159%

Result: Correct.


## Problem 3

A bag has 5 red balls, 4 blue balls, and 3 green balls.

Three balls are drawn without replacement.

Question: What is the probability that exactly 2 of them are red?

Answer: 31.81818181818%

Result: Correct.


## Problem 4

A fair six-sided die is rolled 8 times.

Question: What is the probability of rolling exactly three 6s?

Answer: 10.419%

Result: Correct.


## Problem 5

Two fair dice are rolled.

Question: Given that the sum is at least 9, what is the probability that at least one die is a 6?

Answer: 70%

Result: Correct.


## Problem 6

A game costs $3 to play.

A fair six-sided die is rolled.

If the roll is a 6, the payout is $15.

If the roll is a 4 or 5, the payout is $6.

Otherwise, the payout is $0.

Question: What is the expected profit per play?

Answer: $1.50

Result: Correct.


## Problem 7

A trader makes a prediction each morning: “up” or “down.”

On days when the stock actually goes up, the trader predicts “up” 70% of the time.

On days when the stock actually goes down, the trader still predicts “up” 30% of the time.

Assume the stock goes up on 50% of days and down on 50% of days.

Question: If the trader predicts “up,” what is the probability the stock actually goes up?

Answer: 70%

Result: Correct.


## Problem 8

A market maker buys a contract for $48.

The contract pays $100 if an event happens and $0 otherwise.

The market maker believes the event has a 55% chance of happening.

Question: What is the expected profit from buying the contract?

Answer: $7

Result: Correct.
